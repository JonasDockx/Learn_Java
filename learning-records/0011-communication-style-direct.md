# Feedback: communicate directly, explain instead of reassure

Jonas asked me to stop the human/emotional framing ("you're not behind", "no rush",
"that took honesty") and the morale-management. When he doesn't understand something,
he wants the thing explained, not to be comforted.

**Why:** He finds reassurance to be noise that gets in the way of the technical content.

**How to apply:**
- Lead with the explanation or the next step. No emotional cushioning, no praise padding.
- If he reports confusion, diagnose and explain the concept directly.
- Keep prose lean and technical. State facts, including when something is wrong.
- Still keep the *pace* slow (see [[0010-pace-too-fast-consolidate]]) — that correction
  stands. "Slow pace" and "direct tone" are independent: small steps, plainly stated.
